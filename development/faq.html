Frequently Asked Questions

When is a D-Ostomy most useful? 

The D-Ostomy is most helpful when there is anatomy

(bellybutton, incision, scar, rolls, folds, second ostomy, drains, etc)

that make it hard for a normal appliance to land without overlap.

The straight edge side of the D-ostomy can be placed next to these

challenging areas, allowing the appliance to seal without involving the

anatomy that may have caused issues in the past.

What barriers are available with D-Ostomy?

The D-Ostomy universal, flat, cut-to-fit barrier works

with all D-Ostomy pouches. Cut a hole in the barrier

slightly larger than the stoma, then rotate the barrier

to match the pouch direction. The universal D-Ostomy

barrier can accommodate stomas ranging

in size from 10mm (.39 inches) to 70mm (2.75 inches).

What are the pouch options?

D-Ostomy pouches are available in drainable and closed.  

All D-Ostomy pouches include a filter to help release

gas that may build up in the pouch.
